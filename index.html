EnviroLink: A platform to improve air quality and health of Nairobi people

In alignment with the United Nations' 17 Sustainable Development Goals, our project targets the improvement of "Good Health and Well-being" by addressing the issue of poor air quality, a leading cause of premature death.

The success of our project hinges on four critical elements:

  • Collaborative efforts between government
  • Collaborative efforts between businesses
  • Robust academic research
  • Active public engagement

This problem is particularly concerning in Nairobi, where most of the population faces barriers to healthcare due to socioeconomic constraints. However, a dearth of centralised, reliable data regarding air quality and its health impacts impedes persuasive argumentation to stakeholders.

Our Services

Data Collection and Aggregation

  • Collect existing air quality data for Nairobi from various sources and use sensors to gather localised data.
  • Create a comprehensive database by aggregating all collected data.

Data Visualization and Analysis

  • Visualize air quality data on a map for easy understanding and exploration.
  • Provide historical trends and real-time updates to monitor changes in air pollution.

Health Impact Assessment

  • Assess the health risks associated with air pollution in Nairobi based on scientific research.
  • Generate personalised health risk assessments using location and demographic information.

Policy Recommendations

  • Analyse air quality data, health impact assessments, and best practices to suggest effective policies.
  • Offer specific policy measures such as emissions controls and urban planning guidelines.
