Health Problems due to air Pollution in Nairobi

  • Krishnav Verma
  • Jun 8, 2023

Introduction

Air pollution has caused a significant burden on the health and the livelihood across the continent of East Africa which has a population of more than 1.2 billion people. (Fund, 2023) Nairobi is one of the major cities in the continent affected by air pollution, which is mainly caused by traffic, roadside rubbish fires, road dust, industry, and the use of solid fuels such as charcoal and wood to cook in open fires and leaky stoves (indoor air pollution). (Chasant, 2019)

Health Problems

Air pollution, particularly fine particulate matter (PM2.5) and pollutants like nitrogen dioxide (NO2) and Sulphur dioxide (SO2), can irritate the respiratory system and cause respiratory difficulties. (Vidal, 2016) It has the potential to exacerbate pre-existing illnesses such as asthma, bronchitis, and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). Long-term exposure to polluted air may also raise the likelihood of acquiring respiratory diseases. (Dianti, 2019)

Cardiovascular Diseases: Long-term air pollution exposure has been related to an elevated risk of cardiovascular disease. Pollutants such as carbon monoxide (CO) and nitrogen oxides (NOx) can enter the circulation, causing inflammation and oxidative stress. This can increase the risk of developing heart disease, heart attacks, strokes, and high blood pressure. (Vidal, 2016)

Lung Cancer: Air pollution has been linked to an increased risk of lung cancer, particularly the presence of fine particulate matter and hazardous chemicals such as benzene and formaldehyde. These pollutants can enter deep into the lungs and induce respiratory system damage, potentially leading to the growth of malignant cells. (Vidal, 2016)

Premature Death: The cumulative health impacts of long-term air pollution exposure can lead to premature death. (Egondi, 2013) High levels of air pollution, particularly PM2.5, have been linked to an increased risk of early death from respiratory and cardiovascular disorders, according to research.

Children's Lung Development: Children are more exposed to the health impacts of air pollution. (Egondi, 2013) Early childhood exposure to polluted air can result in decreased lung development, increased respiratory infections, and long-term respiratory difficulties. It may also have an impact on cognitive development and raise the likelihood of neurodevelopmental problems.
